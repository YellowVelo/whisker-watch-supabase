This is exactly the direction I would take for Wysker Watch.

One of the principles we've talked about is that **owners don't diagnose diseases—they observe behavior.** The app should never ask, "Is your dog dehydrated?" It should ask, "Did your dog drink more water today than usual?"

## My recommendation for V1

Rather than presenting owners with 60+ individual questions every day, I'd organize them into **daily check-ins** that take less than a minute:

### 🟢 Daily (30–45 seconds)

* Appetite
* Water intake
* Urination
* Stool
* Vomiting
* Energy
* Activity
* Pain/mobility
* Scratching/itching
* Coughing/breathing
* Mood/behavior
* Medications

### 🟡 Weekly (2–3 minutes)

* Weight
* Body condition
* Lumps/bumps
* Bad breath
* Hair coat
* Nail condition
* Ear odor
* Overall quality of life

### 🔵 Monthly

* Photos (body, teeth, skin, known lumps)
* Mobility assessment
* Dental assessment
* Comprehensive skin check
* Senior wellness questionnaire (for older dogs)

This approach aligns with your vision of making tracking "impossibly easy." Owners answer a handful of behavior-based questions on a regular schedule, and Wysker Watch can detect meaningful patterns across days, weeks, and months instead of overwhelming them with disease-specific forms.
